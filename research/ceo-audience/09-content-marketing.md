# Content Marketing for Selling AI Automation to Non-Technical Business Leaders

Research date: 2026-04-17

---

## 1. YouTube

### What works
Long-form tutorials (10-25 min) showing real business automation builds, screen-recording style. The winning formula is "here is a real business problem, watch me solve it with AI tools you can use without coding." Channels that frame everything around business outcomes (hours saved, costs cut) outperform those that lead with technology.

### Tone
Conversational, operator-to-operator. The best performers are themselves business owners who automated their own companies, not developers teaching tech. First-person experience ("I automated my DJ company") beats abstract advice.

### CTAs that convert
Free workflow templates, community access (Skool groups), and accelerator programs. Liam Ottley's AAA Accelerator (3,800+ founders) grew entirely through YouTube content -- screen recordings of builds + agency scaling advice. Lead magnets are downloadable automations, not ebooks.

### Effort
High. 1-2 videos/week, each requiring a real build or case study. But compounds fast -- evergreen "how to automate X" videos keep pulling traffic for months.

### Who does it well
- **Liam Ottley** (568K subs, 20M views) -- AI automation agency model, practical builds
- **Jono Catliff** -- real business operator (Canada's largest DJ/photo/video company), automated his own operations, most relatable for non-technical owners
- **Sabrina Ramonov** -- AI prompts, agent systems, and automation playbooks for solopreneurs
- **Nate Herk** -- AI agents for email, leads, business processes, no-code focus
- **The AI Advantage** -- short, focused no-code automation tutorials (Make.com, Zapier)
- **AI Automation Station** -- N8N, Make.com, Zapier automation builds
- **Grace Leung** -- AI tools for digital marketers/consultants with business outcome framing

## 2. Blog / SEO Content

### What ranks
The top-ranking content for "automate business with AI" falls into three categories:

1. **Tool roundups** -- "30 Best AI Marketing Tools in 2026" (Marketer Milk). Listicles with specific tool names, pricing, use cases. High search volume, moderate competition.
2. **Trend analysis** -- "AI Automation Trends for 2026" (Salesmate, Klaviyo). Framework-style pieces that help leaders understand what changed and what to prioritize.
3. **ROI-focused guides** -- "AI-Powered Marketing Automation: Proven Strategies, Real Results" (ALM Corp). Data-heavy, outcome-first articles that answer "what will this actually do for my business."

### Angle that wins
Business problem first, AI second. Articles that open with a pain point ("your support team is drowning in tickets") outperform articles that open with technology ("GPT-4 can now..."). The framing is always: you are falling behind if you are not doing this.

### Tone
Authoritative but accessible. No jargon. Numbers everywhere -- percentages, dollar amounts, time saved. The content reads like a consulting brief, not a tech blog.

### CTAs that convert
Free audit / assessment tools, ROI calculators, downloadable frameworks. The best-performing posts end with "book a free automation audit" rather than "subscribe to our newsletter."

### Effort
Medium-high for quality SEO content. 1-2 long-form posts per week (2,000-3,000 words). Tool roundups need updating quarterly. But organic traffic compounds and has the best long-term CAC.

### Who does it well
- **Zapier blog** -- always ties AI tools back to workflows and business outcomes
- **Klaviyo blog** -- marketing automation trends with data-backed claims
- **Demand Gen Report** -- B2B-specific AI adoption, enterprise-level framing

## 3. LinkedIn

### What works
LinkedIn's 2026 algorithm rewards "Depth Score" -- dwell time and conversational depth are the #1 ranking factors. This favors long, story-driven posts over quick tips.

**Top-performing formats:**
- Carousels / document posts (6.6% engagement rate) -- 8-12 slides, bold headlines, one actionable takeaway. Highest ROI format for agencies.
- Polls (8.9% engagement rate)
- Video posts (5.1%)
- Personal story posts from the founder's personal account -- "A CEO sharing a lesson learned gets read; a logo posting a press release gets ignored."

**What gets penalized:**
- External links in the main post (60% reach reduction)
- Engagement bait ("Comment YES if you agree") -- actively throttled
- Generic motivational content (under 1% engagement)

### Tone
Human, first-person, specific. Niche industry-specific content gets 15-22% ICP-fit engagement vs. under 1% for generic. Posts should read like a conversation between peers, not marketing copy.

### CTAs that convert
"DM me [keyword] for the template" works for lead gen. Self-contained carousels that demonstrate expertise drive inbound. Comment-driven engagement (asking genuine questions) outperforms link-sharing.

### Effort
Medium. 3-5 posts/week from a personal (not company) account. Carousel creation is the highest-ROI activity. Note: organic reach dropped ~50% YoY, so consistency matters more than ever.

### Who does it well
- AI automation agency founders posting builds and results from personal accounts
- Operators sharing "before/after" automation stories with specific metrics
- Founders documenting their own automation journey in real-time

### Critical note
LinkedIn AI detection accuracy is 97% for obvious automation. Authentic, human-written content is non-negotiable. AI-assisted drafting is fine; AI-generated posting is detectable and penalized.

## 4. Podcasts

### What works
Interview format dominates. The best business-AI podcasts bring on operators who implemented AI, not researchers who built it. Non-technical leaders want to hear from peers who faced the same decisions.

### Tone
Conversational, interview-driven. Humor helps (AI For Humans succeeds with entertainment + education). Jargon-free. The best hosts translate technical concepts into business language in real-time.

### CTAs that convert
Guest appearances on established podcasts are the highest-leverage play for an AI agency. Being a guest on 2-3 business podcasts per month builds authority faster than starting your own. If starting your own: focus on client interviews (they become case studies and referral generators).

### Effort
**Being a guest:** Low-medium. Pitch 5-10 shows/month, prep 30 min per appearance.
**Running your own:** High. Weekly recording, editing, promotion. Takes 6-12 months to build meaningful listenership.

### Key podcasts for the audience
- **The AI in Business Podcast** (Emerj) -- specifically for non-technical business leaders, Fortune 500 AI executives and startup founders, practical adoption
- **Everyday AI Podcast** -- business leaders staying current, context over technical depth
- **Agents of Scale** -- hosted by Zapier CEO Wade Foster, C-suite conversations about real AI transformation
- **The Cognitive Revolution** -- broad audience including business leaders and policymakers
- **AI For Humans** -- accessible, entertaining, uses demonstrations and stories over jargon

## 5. Case Studies

### Format that works
The winning structure is Problem-Solution-Result with hard numbers:

1. **Specific pain point** -- "5-person support team handling 400 tickets/day, 48-hour response times, 62% satisfaction score"
2. **What was built** -- describe the automation in business terms, not technical terms. "AI agent integrated with Shopify and Zendesk" not "RAG pipeline with vector embeddings"
3. **Quantified results** -- "Bot handled 60% of inquiries without human intervention. 30% reduction in support costs. Satisfaction up to 89%."
4. **Timeline** -- how long from kickoff to live. Business leaders care about speed to value.

### Key principles
- Generic testimonials are dead. "We saved Company X $180K/year by automating their intake process" beats "Great to work with, highly recommend."
- Customer's voice throughout -- let them tell the story, not you.
- Package around outcomes, not features. "We cut their hiring process from 3 weeks to 4 days" not "We built a custom AI agent using Claude."
- Visual aids: before/after screenshots, charts showing metric changes, process flow diagrams.

### CTAs that convert
Case studies work best as mid-funnel content. CTA is "book a free automation audit to find your version of this" or "see if this applies to your business." Not a hard sell -- an invitation to explore.

### Effort
Medium. 1-2 case studies per month from real client work. Each requires client cooperation (getting approval, gathering metrics). But they are the single most effective content type for B2B service sales.

### Who does it well
- **MEWR Creative** -- "5 AI Automation Case Studies: Real Businesses, Real Results" format
- **Beam AI** -- dedicated case study section organized by industry
- **EY** -- enterprise-level case studies with specific metric improvements
- Case studies presented on landing pages with hero section, problem agitation, solution, and results sections

## 6. Instagram / TikTok (Short-Form Video)

### What works
Short-form video for AI automation is viable but requires a specific approach. The audience on these platforms skews younger and more entrepreneurial than LinkedIn -- think small business owners, freelancers, and aspiring agency founders.

**Formats that perform:**
- Screen recordings of automations running (30-60 sec) -- "Watch this AI handle 50 customer emails in 2 minutes"
- Before/after comparisons -- "This used to take 3 hours. Now it takes 3 seconds."
- "I automated [specific task] and here's what happened" story format
- Quick tool demos with business context

### Tone
Fast, visual, outcome-driven. No long explanations -- show, don't tell. Hook in first 2 seconds. TikTok's 2026 algorithm rewards authentic, human-feeling content over polished production.

### CTAs that convert
"Link in bio" to a free resource, audit booking page, or community. Short-form video is top-of-funnel -- it drives awareness, not direct sales. Funnel: short video -> profile -> link -> lead magnet -> email list -> sales conversation.

### Effort
Medium-low per piece but requires volume. 3-5 short videos/week minimum. Can repurpose from YouTube long-form (chop key moments into Reels/Shorts). AI tools (Spikes Studio, quso.ai) can automate the chopping.

### Realistic assessment
This channel is better for building personal brand and audience than for direct client acquisition. CEOs and senior business leaders are less active here than on LinkedIn. Best used as a supplementary channel, not primary. Works well for solopreneurs and SMB owners under 45.

### Who does it well
- **Liam Ottley** on TikTok -- repurposes YouTube content into short clips
- Faceless AI automation channels using screen recordings + voiceover
- Business owners documenting their automation journey in real-time

## 7. Email Newsletter

### What works
The highest-converting B2B AI newsletters share three traits:
1. **Genuine utility** -- readers can make decisions based on the content (not just stay informed)
2. **Personal voice** -- from a named human, not a brand. Personal newsletters convert 5-25% vs. branded newsletters at lower rates.
3. **Structured, repeatable format** -- readers know what to expect each issue

**Winning format for an AI agency:**
- Personal intro (2-3 sentences, what happened this week)
- 1 deep-dive: a real automation build, case study, or lesson learned
- 3-5 curated links to relevant AI business news
- 1 actionable tip readers can implement today
- CTA: "Reply to this email if [specific question]" -- drives replies, which improves deliverability

### Critical 2026 shift: AI gatekeepers
Email clients now use AI to filter and summarize incoming mail. Generic "just checking in" or broad product announcements never reach the human. Emails need "High-Signal" traits: technical specificity (precise data points), relevance to the recipient's industry, and clear utility.

### The plain-text advantage
The highest-converting B2B emails look like they were typed by a colleague on their phone. Short, plain-text, no HTML templates. Intentional informality (missing capitals, "Sent from my iPhone" signatures) signals authenticity.

### Frequency
Bi-weekly or weekly. Monthly is too infrequent to build habit; daily is too aggressive for B2B.

### CTAs that convert
- "Reply to this email" (highest engagement, best for deliverability)
- "Book a 15-min automation audit" (direct conversion)
- Free resource downloads tied to the deep-dive topic

### Effort
Medium. 2-4 hours per issue if you are doing real work (building automations, serving clients) and writing about it. The content comes from the work itself, not from research. This is the most sustainable channel for a solo operator.

### Reference newsletters (format inspiration)
- **Superhuman AI** (1M+ subs) -- "Get smarter about AI in 3 minutes." Quick hits + tool of the day + prompts. High-volume, low-depth.
- **Ben's Bites** (120K+ subs) -- casual commentary, curated links, AI business ecosystem focus. Best model for a solo operator's voice.
- **TLDR AI** (1.25M readers) -- technical filter, distills complex news into short summaries. More dev-oriented but format is instructive.
- **The Rundown AI** (2M+ subs) -- daily, broad coverage. Shows the power of consistency.

## Recommended Stack for a Solo AI Consultant

**Primary channels (start here):**
1. **LinkedIn personal account** -- 3-5 posts/week, carousels showing automation builds, personal stories. Fastest path to CEO-level prospects.
2. **Email newsletter** -- weekly, plain-text, personal voice. Write about what you built this week. Ben's Bites format.
3. **Case studies** -- 1-2/month as you complete client work. Most powerful conversion content.

**Secondary channels (add at month 2-3):**
4. **Podcast guesting** -- pitch 5-10 business podcasts/month. Leverage other people's audiences.
5. **YouTube** -- 1 video/week, screen recording of a real build. Repurpose into LinkedIn carousels and short-form clips.

**Tertiary (only if you have capacity):**
6. **Blog/SEO** -- long-form posts repurposed from YouTube scripts or newsletter deep-dives.
7. **TikTok/Instagram** -- chop YouTube into Reels/Shorts using AI tools.

## Universal Principles Across All Channels

1. **Business outcomes first, technology second.** Never lead with "AI" or "Claude" or "GPT." Lead with "cut your support costs 30%" or "hire 3x faster." The technology is the how, not the what.

2. **Show, don't tell.** Screen recordings of working automations beat explanations. Before/after metrics beat promises. Client quotes beat self-promotion.

3. **Personal > brand.** A named human with opinions and experience outperforms a company page on every platform in 2026. Post from your personal account, not a logo.

4. **Specificity wins.** "We saved a 12-person accounting firm $180K/year by automating their invoice processing" beats "We help businesses leverage AI for efficiency." Niche industry-specific content gets 15-22x the engagement of generic content.

5. **Plain language is a competitive advantage.** Most AI content is written by and for technical people. Writing for business leaders in their language (ROI, headcount, speed, risk) is an underserved angle.

6. **Repurpose ruthlessly.** One client automation build becomes: a YouTube video, a LinkedIn carousel, a newsletter deep-dive, 3 TikTok clips, a case study, and a podcast talking point. One build, seven pieces of content.
