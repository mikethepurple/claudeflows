# Vibecoder Ecosystem: Who Is Selling to Non-Developer AI Builders?

**Research date:** 2026-04-17
**Scope:** Courses, communities, consulting, products, influencers, events, and marketplaces targeting vibecoders (non-developer users of AI coding tools)

---

## Market Context

- **r/vibecoding** has 153K+ members (grew from 0 to 153K in ~1 year; r/webdev took a decade for similar scale)
- 63% of active r/vibecoding users are non-developers (PMs, founders, marketers, operators)
- Collins Dictionary named "vibe coding" Word of the Year 2025
- MIT Technology Review named it one of "10 Breakthrough Technologies of 2026"
- Term coined by Andrej Karpathy (Feb 2025): "fully give in to the vibes, embrace exponentials, forget the code exists"

## Competitive Landscape Summary

### What IS being sold to vibecoders:

1. **Courses** (biggest category) - Udemy ($10-50), Coursera ($49/mo), Maven cohorts ($300-800), niche courses ($200-300)
2. **Communities** - Discord/Circle, mostly free or low-cost ($10-30/mo), some lifetime
3. **Security audits** - $2,500-30,000 per engagement, fragmented market
4. **Freelance building** - Upwork/Fiverr, $50-200/hr, no productized offerings
5. **Books** - $10-35, traditional publishing catching up
6. **Free content** - YouTube, newsletters, blogs (monetized via ads/affiliates)

### What is NOT being sold (gaps):

1. **Productized consulting** - Nobody sells "Claude Code Sprint: $X for Y deliverable in Z days"
2. **Build + teach hybrid** - Courses teach, freelancers build, nobody does both in one engagement
3. **Ongoing support** - No retainer model for "I vibecoded this, now keep it running"
4. **Bundled security** - Audit is always a separate, expensive purchase
5. **Claude Code-specific premium content** - Most paid courses are tool-agnostic or Cursor-focused
6. **Non-technical founder community with builder access** - Communities are social, not commercial
7. **Skill storefront for non-devs** - Vercel's skills.sh is developer-to-developer

### Price sensitivity signals:

- Vibecoders already pay $20/mo for Claude Pro/Max
- Udemy courses sell at $10-50 (high volume, low price)
- Maven cohorts sell at $300-800 (moderate volume, moderate price)
- Security audits sell at $2,500+ (low volume, high price)
- Freelance Claude work goes for $50-200/hr on Upwork
- One marketer reported building a EUR 60-70K app after a EUR 200 course

### CuratedFlows positioning opportunity:

The market has **educators** (courses, books, YouTube) and **doers** (freelancers, audit firms) but nobody combines both. The "I'll build it with you and teach you along the way" model is the white space. This is exactly the Sprint model: fixed-price, fixed-scope, done-with-you.
