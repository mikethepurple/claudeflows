# Search Query Validation: Live Google Results Analysis

**Date:** 2026-04-17
**Method:** Searched 30 top queries + 10 net-new queries on Google via web search. Analyzed organic results, advertiser presence, content types, and intent alignment.

---

## Executive Summary

The search landscape for AI automation + small business is **saturated with content but light on ads** for most queries. The big players (Zapier, Make, Salesforce, HubSpot) dominate organic results for tool-discovery queries. The consulting/agency space is fragmented -- dozens of small agencies compete, but none own the SERP. The biggest opportunity for CuratedFlows is in **"done for you" and "hire someone to set up AI" queries**, where intent is high and competition is mostly small agencies with weak landing pages.

**Key finding:** The word "sprint" already appears in competitor offerings (MIT Sloan AI Sprint, Design Sprints Studio AI Sprint, "AI Setup Sprint"). This validates the sprint framing but means CuratedFlows needs differentiation beyond just calling it a sprint.

## CRITICAL FINDINGS

### 1. "Done for you" is the money phrase
The phrase "done for you AI automation" returns an entire SERP of small agencies with weak positioning. This is the single highest-opportunity query cluster for CuratedFlows.

### 2. Pricing transparency is a weapon
Almost every agency hides pricing behind "contact us." Queries like "AI automation agency pricing" and "how much does AI automation cost" have no authoritative, clear-priced results. CuratedFlows' sprint pricing (fixed scope, fixed price) would stand out immediately.

### 3. The "sprint" framing is validated but not unique
MIT Sloan has a "Generative AI Business Sprint." Design Sprints Studio has an "AI Design Sprint." A Vercel app called "AI Setup Sprint" exists. The sprint concept is validated but CuratedFlows needs a differentiator: the combination of sprint + Claude-specific + done-with-you.

### 4. Claude-specific queries are underserved
"How to use Claude AI for my business" returns educational blog posts but NO service providers offering to implement Claude-based workflows. This is a wide-open niche that CuratedFlows should own immediately.

### 5. Google AI Overviews are eating top-of-funnel
Many problem-aware queries trigger AI Overviews that answer the question directly. Users don't click through. This makes bottom-of-funnel (ready-to-buy) queries even more valuable for paid ads.

### 6. The VA industry is fighting back
"Can AI replace my virtual assistant" returns a wall of VA companies arguing AI can't replace humans. This is a content battleground where CuratedFlows should NOT compete directly but can use the "AI + human" angle.
