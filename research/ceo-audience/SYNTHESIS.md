## 1. Executive Summary

Five findings that matter. Everything else is detail.

**Finding 1: The market is real but the easy money is gone.** AI automation consulting for SMBs is a $14B-$30B global market growing 26-36% annually (File 13). 57% of SMBs rely on external providers for AI implementation (IDC 2026). But 95% of AI pilots fail (MIT), 42% of companies scrapped AI initiatives in 2025 (S&P Global), and buyers now demand measurable ROI before spending. The "demo-and-disappear" era is over. You must sell outcomes, not automation. (Files 01, 11, 13, 16)

**Finding 2: Target recruiting/staffing agencies first, not marketing agencies.** The original research ranked marketing agencies #1 (File 07, score 9.2/10). Counter-evidence and niche validation reversed this. Marketing agencies are financially squeezed (only 14% describe their pipeline as "very healthy"), can DIY most automations, and the space is flooded with "AI automation bro" competitors. Recruiting agencies face an existential threat (AI letting companies hire without them), live on LinkedIn (your primary channel), make fast decisions, and have clear ROI math ($2K/mo retainer saves one recruiter 10 hrs/week = $2,500/mo in labor). No dominant "done-for-you AI automation for staffing agencies" player was found. (Files 16, 19, 20)

**Finding 3: Google Ads at $20/day beats Meta at $15/day.** $15/day on Meta is structurally below the minimum needed for B2B lead generation -- Meta recommends $150+/day, and you cannot exit the learning phase at this budget. Google Ads captures people already searching for help ("done for you AI automation," "someone to set up AI for my business"). At $20/day with validated keywords, expect 3-5 leads/month and 1-2 clients/month at $1,500-5,000 each. Use Meta only for $3-5/day retargeting starting Month 3. (Files 08, 12, 16, 23)

**Finding 4: Realistic Year 1 income is $5K-$10K/month, not $15K-$100K.** The research cited solo operators earning $15K-$100K/month (Files 07, 13). Counter-evidence found severe survivorship bias: 80-95% of AI projects fail, the $4.5M ARR claim is unverified, and median Year 1 for AI consultants is $3K-$8K/month (AIFire.co trajectory). Plan for $5K-$10K/month by Month 6 with a single vertical focus. The upside exists but is a Year 2 outcome. (File 16)

**Finding 5: Keep "CuratedFlows" as domain but fix the positioning.** "Curated" was voted #1 worst jargon word of 2020 (The Buzzsaw). "Flows" is platform jargon -- CEOs say "set it up," "save time," "scale without hiring," not "flows." But the domain is purchased and clean. Solution: use CuratedFlows.com as the website, lead all marketing with "Done-for-you AI automation" tagline, and never let "Curated Flows" stand alone without a plain-English descriptor. (File 21)

## 2. Target Audience

### Primary: Recruiting/Staffing Agencies (Attack First)

**Profile:** Owner or managing director of a 5-20 person staffing agency. Revenue $1M-$10M. Uses 3-5 disconnected tools (ATS + CRM + LinkedIn + job boards + outreach). Faces existential pressure: AI is letting companies recruit directly without agencies.

**Why first:**
- Existential urgency creates the fastest buying cycle of any niche (File 20)
- 70% of businesses will use AI to hire by 2026; 82% already depend on AI for resume screening (File 07)
- They live on LinkedIn -- both your sales channel AND your proving ground (File 15)
- Clear ROI: 1 recruiter + AI agents = 3x previous capacity. $2K/mo retainer saves $2,500/mo in labor (File 20)
- 20,000 staffing agencies in the US -- focused enough to specialize, large enough to sustain (File 07)
- No dominant "done-for-you" competitor found. SaaS tools exist (Manatal $15/user/mo, Workable $189/mo) but they need someone to set up, connect, and customize them (File 20)

**What they need automated:** Resume screening and scoring, candidate outreach sequences, interview scheduling, pipeline tracking across ATS + CRM, client reporting (placements, time-to-fill metrics).

**Budget:** $2,500-$8,000 one-time setup + $1,000-$2,500/mo retainer. Compares against hiring another recruiter at $55-75K/year. (Files 07, 10, 20)

**How to reach:** LinkedIn outreach (20 personalized messages/day), LinkedIn organic content (3-5 posts/week showing before/after automation results), Google Ads on "AI automation for staffing agencies."

### Secondary: Home Services Businesses (Expand at Month 3-4)

**Profile:** "Tradesman Tony" -- owner of a plumbing, HVAC, electrical, or landscaping company. Revenue $500K-$3M, 5-20 employees. Validated strongest of all personas (File 19). 85% of callers who reach voicemail call a competitor. Missing 4 calls/week at $1,200/job = $250K/year lost.

**Why second (not first):** Requires Facebook groups and referrals to reach (slower than LinkedIn). Lower ticket ($500-$2,000 setup). Voice AI receptionist setup requires Twilio infrastructure -- different skillset than workflow automation. But the pain is acute and the competition (83% of landscaping pros haven't adopted AI) is nearly zero in Google Ads. (Files 19, 20, 23)

**Budget:** $500-$2,000 setup + $200-$400/month. Frame against cost of one lost job. (File 05)

### Tertiary: Professional Services Ops Leaders (Expand at Month 4-6)

**Profile:** "Operations Omar" -- COO or Head of Ops at a 20-80 person services company. Tools don't talk to each other. Spends Friday afternoon compiling reports from 5 different systems. Has budget authority up to $5K without board approval. (File 05)

**Budget:** $2,000-$5,000 setup. Frames as "process improvement," not "AI." (File 05)

### Skip for Now
- **Marketing agencies:** Oversaturated, financially squeezed, can DIY (Files 16, 20)
- **Real estate agents:** Buy $299/mo SaaS (Ylopo, Lofty), not $2K consulting (File 20)
- **E-commerce:** Shopify native AI + Tidio at $29/mo solves 80% of the problem (File 20)
- **Startup founders:** Most will DIY; weakest validated persona (File 19)

## 6. Meta Ads Campaign

### Budget Reality
$15/day ($450/month) is below Meta's recommended $150+/day for B2B lead generation. The algorithm cannot exit the learning phase at this budget. (File 16)

**Revised approach:** Do NOT use Meta as a primary lead channel. Use it as:
1. **Brand awareness experiment** (Month 1-2): $0-5/day, traffic objective, testing creative
2. **Retargeting only** (Month 3+): $3-5/day retargeting website visitors from Google Ads

### Campaign Structure (Month 3+)
```
Campaign: CuratedFlows Retargeting
  Objective: Traffic (Landing Page Views)
  Budget: $3-5/day
  
  Ad Set: Website Visitors
    Audience: Custom audience -- all website visitors (180 days)
    Exclude: People who already booked a call
    Placements: Advantage+ (let Meta optimize)
    
    Ad 1: Testimonial/case study video (15 sec)
    Ad 2: Carousel (problem > proof > solution > CTA)
    Ad 3: Static image with client result metric
```

### Creative Concepts (validated, scored 8.0-8.6 composite, File 22)

**Run first (highest scores):**
1. "The DIY Graveyard" -- 15-sec video: "You tried ChatGPT. You tried Zapier. Nothing stuck." Cross-persona, strongest emotional hook. Score: 8.6
2. "The 95% Stat" -- Carousel: "95% of AI projects fail. Here's why yours won't." Trust-builder, best for retargeting. Score: 8.2

**Run for specific verticals:**
3. "The Missed Calls Calculator" (home services) -- Static: "You Missed 4 Calls Today. That's $4,800 This Week." Score: 8.4
4. "The Friday Afternoon Report" (ops leaders) -- Video: "Still Compiling Reports on Friday Afternoon?" Score: 8.4
5. "Speed to Lead" (real estate, if expanding) -- Video: "Your Agents Respond in 4 Hours. Their Competitor Responds in 4 Minutes." Score: 8.6

**Killed concepts:**
- "AI Is the Future" thought leadership (under 1% engagement, File 22)
- "Watch Me Build" screen recording (alienates non-technical audience, File 22)
- "Replace Your VA" pitch (synonymous with AI agency scams, File 22)

### Static vs Video Resolution (Contradiction #3)

**Resolution:** Both claims are partially correct, from different contexts. Static images drive 60-70% of *direct-response* conversions measured by CPC efficiency. Video generates leads at $2.75 vs $14.22 for static in *engagement-optimized* campaigns. (Files 04, 16)

**What to do:** At $3-5/day retargeting budget, test one video + one static + one carousel. Let Meta optimize. Video is likely the winner for retargeting (where engagement matters) but static works for direct-response CTAs. Do not commit to either format -- let data decide within 2 weeks.

## 9. Sales Process

### The Full Funnel: First Touch to Closed Deal

**Source:** File 17 identified this as the #1 gap in all 23 files. 96% of SMB purchases are made by the CEO alone, deals close in 30-60 days, and proposals sent within 24 hours close 35% faster.

**Step 1: Attraction (Day 0)**
- Prospect sees LinkedIn post, Google Ad, or referral
- Clicks through to /audit or /staffing landing page
- Fills out form: Name, Email, Business Type, Biggest Time Waster

**Step 2: Free Audit Call (Day 1-3)**
- 30-minute video call. You screen-share and walk through their current workflow.
- Identify top 3 automatable processes with time/dollar savings for each.
- Deliver a one-page "Automation Opportunity Map" by email same day.
- This IS the demo. Make it actionable enough that they can say yes on the spot. (File 17)

**Step 3: Proposal (Day 1-2 after audit)**
- Send same-day or next-day. A 24-hour delay costs 35% in close rate. (File 17)
- One page. Contains: scope (which workflows), timeline (2 weeks), price (fixed), guarantee.
- Accept credit cards (Stripe). If the CEO has to cut a check or get invoice approval, the deal dies. (File 17)

**Step 4: Kickoff (Day 3-7 after proposal)**
- Client signs + pays via Stripe link in the proposal.
- Schedule kickoff call within 48 hours.
- Get access to their tools (ATS, CRM, email, scheduling).

**Step 5: Sprint Delivery (Days 7-21)**
- Build automations. Show progress via shared Loom recordings.
- "Done with you" where possible: client watches, learns, builds trust.
- Mid-sprint check-in at Day 10.

**Step 6: Handoff + Training (Day 21-28)**
- Live training session (recorded for their team).
- Documentation of what was built and how it works.
- 30-day support window included.

**Step 7: Retainer Upsell (Day 30+)**
- Monthly optimization call: "Here's what your automations did this month. Here's what we could add next."
- Research-as-service upsell: $299/month competitor intelligence briefing (File 14).
- Target: 40-60% of sprint clients convert to retainer.

| Home services owner | Owner alone | 1 week (referral), 3-4 weeks (cold) | Credit card |

## 10. Pricing Strategy

### Core Offerings

| Tier | What | Price | Target |
|------|------|-------|--------|
| **Free Audit** | 30-min call + Automation Opportunity Map | $0 | Lead qualification |
| **Starter Sprint** | 1 workflow automated + training | $2,500 one-time | First-time buyers, home services |
| **Growth Sprint** | 3 connected workflows + training + 30-day support | $5,000 one-time | Staffing agencies, ops leaders |
| **Scale Sprint** | Full pipeline automation + reporting + training | $8,000 one-time | Larger staffing/services firms |
| **Monthly Optimization** | Ongoing improvements, new automations, support | $1,000-$2,500/mo | Sprint graduates |
| **Research-as-Service** | Weekly competitor intelligence briefing | $299/mo | Any vertical, retention play |

### Pricing Rationale

- **Floor: $2,500 (not $997).** The counter-evidence found the $2,500 gap may be structurally unprofitable below this -- clients with $997 budgets expect $5,000 worth of work. Successful practitioners price at $4,500-$7,500 setup. (Files 10, 16)
- **Ceiling: $8,000 per sprint.** Above this, you're competing with agencies that have teams and case studies. Stay below agency pricing until you have 5+ case studies.
- **Value framing:** Price at 10-25% of the annual financial impact. A $5,000 sprint that saves $60,000/year in recruiter time = 12x ROI. (File 10)
- **Retainer model:** Sprint is the wedge; retainer is the business model. 4 of 7 validated personas would convert to $200-800/month retainer after a successful setup. (File 05)

### Framing That Works

- Lead with dollar amount saved, not hours. "Saves $3,000/month" > "saves 15 hours/week." (File 10)
- Anchor against the alternative: "This sprint costs $5,000. A full-time recruiter costs $65,000/year."
- Include guarantee: "If we don't save you 10+ hours in the first month, full refund."
- Show working demos -- eliminate the imagination gap for automation-unfamiliar clients. (File 10)

### Days 61-90: Scale What Works

**Goal:** $5K-$10K monthly revenue, 2-3 active clients, retainer pipeline forming.

| Week | Actions | Metrics |
|------|---------|---------|
| 9-10 | Upsell first Sprint client to monthly retainer. Build referral program ($250-$500 per closed deal). Pitch 5 business podcasts for guest appearances. Increase Google Ads to $25-30/day if ROAS is positive. | 1+ retainer client, referral program live |

**Kill gate (Day 90):** If total revenue is under $5K after 90 days of full-time effort, the model is not working as a standalone business at this stage. Options: (a) pivot niche, (b) reduce to part-time and supplement with Upwork gigs for cash flow, (c) double down on the specific vertical that showed the most traction.

## 12. Risks & Mitigations

### Risk 1: AI Tools Get So Easy That "Done-For-You" Becomes Obsolete
**Probability:** Medium (12-24 month timeline). 70% of new business apps now use low-code/no-code (Gartner). Platforms like Replit and Durable generate working apps from plain English.
**Mitigation:** Shift positioning from "I build automations" to "I redesign your workflows AND implement the right automations." The strategy/audit layer is defensible even if implementation becomes trivial. Monitor quarterly: when a new tool makes a specific automation easy, drop it from the menu and replace with the next complexity layer. (File 17)

### Risk 2: Burned Buyers Kill Conversion Rates
**Probability:** High. 95% of AI pilots fail (MIT). 42% of companies scrapped AI in 2025. Reddit is full of skepticism about AI consultants.
**Mitigation:** Lead with the MIT stat ("DIY: 33% success. Specialist: 67%"). Include money-back guarantee in every proposal. Start with a tiny pilot ($2,500 for 1 workflow), prove it works, then expand. Case studies with real numbers are the cure -- build them obsessively from Day 1. (Files 11, 16, 22)

### Risk 3: Google Ads Burn Cash Without Converting
**Probability:** Medium. B2B consulting CPCs are $5-15 and AI Overviews eat top-of-funnel clicks. Month 1 is learning, not earning.
**Mitigation:** Start with zero-competition keywords ($2-5 CPC) alongside bottom-funnel. Review search terms daily for 14 days. Kill keywords with 50+ clicks and 0 conversions. Set $12 max CPC. Accept that Month 1 is data-gathering, not lead-gen. Break-even requires 1 client/month at $2,500+ against $600 ad spend. (Files 08, 23)

### Risk 4: Solo Operator Capacity Ceiling
**Probability:** High at Month 4-6. A done-with-you sprint requires synchronous time with each client. 3-4 sprints/month max = $10K-$20K/month revenue ceiling. (File 16)
**Mitigation:** (a) Retainers scale better than sprints (monitoring 10 clients takes less time than building for 4). (b) Productize: build templates for the most common automations in your niche so delivery takes 1 week instead of 2. (c) At $15K+/month, hire a part-time contractor for implementation while you focus on sales and strategy.

### Risk 5: Platform Risk (Anthropic/OpenAI Enter Consulting)
**Probability:** Low for SMB market. Anthropic is partnering with McKinsey, BCG, and Accenture for enterprise ($100K+ engagements). Big Four cannot profitably serve $2K-$10K deals. (File 17)
**Mitigation:** Use their enterprise presence as a trust signal: "We use the same AI that Accenture deploys for Fortune 500 companies -- but sized for your business." Never position as competing with Big Four. Monitor annually for downmarket moves.
