## Technical Gaps (raw findings)

**Payment processing comparison:**

Well-researched in synthesis but critical detail missing:
- Lemon Squeezy: 5% + $0.50 per transaction (merchant of record, handles taxes/VAT globally as of 2026)
- Gumroad: 10% + payment processing (~3% + 30¢), switched to merchant of record January 2025
- Polar: 4% per transaction (newest, lowest fees, no monthly fee)
- Stripe: 2.9% + $0.30 (if you wire it yourself, no platform cut, but you handle taxes, file delivery, customer management)
- Stripe announced private beta merchant of record solution (Q2 2026) with expected 3.5% additional fee

**What's missing:**
- No research on Paddle as payment processor (mentioned in Tolt affiliate software comparison but not evaluated)
- Zero data on tax compliance requirements by country for sellers (beyond Lemon Squeezy/Gumroad handling it)
- No research on chargeback rates, fraud prevention, or dispute resolution across platforms

**DRM/IP protection for SKILL.md files:**

- Generic DRM concepts found (encryption, license keys, activation, DMCA protection) but zero specific to:
  - How to protect .md files (plain text, easily copyable)
  - Whether DRM is even practical for open-format skills
  - How competitors (Claw Mart, Agent37) handle IP protection (if at all)
  - SKILL.md spec does it enforce any DRM standards? (no evidence found)

**Skill usage tracking/analytics:**

- Claude Code Analytics API documented (tracks lines of code, acceptance rates, contribution metrics, skill usage per user)
- Enterprise organizations can track skill adoption via analytics
- Datadog offers Claude Code monitoring, Faros offers ROI measurement
- No research on whether third-party skill creators (on ClaudeFlows, Agent37, Claw Mart) can track usage of their individual skills
- No data on whether skill usage metrics are exposed to creators for marketing purposes

## Summary: What Looks Thin

**Critical data gaps the research didn't cover (ordered by visibility to business decisions):**

1. **Non-technical buyer persona** — No research on what keywords they search for, how they discover tools, their price sensitivity, or whether they even exist as a viable segment for Claude Code skills. The synthesis assumes they exist; validation is missing.

2. **Enterprise skill procurement workflows** — Zero data on decision-making processes, approvals, team sizes, or typical project scopes for paid consulting.

3. **Affiliate/referral economics for one-time products** — SaaS affiliate data is rich but digital product affiliate programs (if they exist) are undocumented.

4. **International markets (non-English, non-US/UK)** — Payment methods, communities, skill supply, adoption rates completely unmapped.

5. **Video/course bundling strategy** — Whether "skill + video walkthrough" as a bundle product actually converts better than skill-only is untested.

6. **DRM/IP protection for SKILL.md format** — No strategy exists or has been researched for how solo creators protect plain-text code files.

7. **Individual skill creator analytics/tracking** — Unclear whether third-party marketplace creators can measure their own skill adoption and usage for marketing.

8. **Competitor affiliate programs** — Do Agent37, Claw Mart, SkillHub offer affiliates? Unknown.

9. **Anthropic's actual paid skills plans** — No public roadmap. Market window assumption is based on silence, not evidence.

10. **Video-only content monetization** — Whether creators are successfully selling Claude Code video tutorials on Loom, Vimeo, or other platforms as standalone products is unexplored.
