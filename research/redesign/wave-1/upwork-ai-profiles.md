## Patterns Across Successful Profiles

### 1. Title Formula
All high-performing profiles follow: **[Primary Role] | [Tool Stack] | [Niche or Differentiator]**

Examples observed:
- "AI Systems Engineer | n8n, VAPI, Claude Code | Workflow Automation"
- "n8n Automation Expert | AI Agent Developer | Make, GHL & Claude Code"
- "AI Automation Expert | N8N | Python | Claude code | clawdbot"
- "Claude Code Expert & AI Developer & Data Scientist"

Tool names in title = keyword-matching for clients who search by tool. "Claude Code" specifically is a searchable differentiator since few profiles hold it.

### 2. Bio Opening (First 200 Characters)
Every successful bio front-loads the **client outcome**, not credentials. The algorithm weights the first 200 characters more heavily for indexing.

Pattern: "I help [type of business] [achieve specific outcome] using [specific tools]."

What does NOT work: "I am an experienced AI developer with X years..."

### 3. Outcome Numbers in Bio
Top profiles all include specific business metrics:
- "40–70% overhead reduction"
- "93 additional appointments in one week"
- "15x–20x reduction in time and money requirements"
- "$1M in client automations in 2025"
- "Lead booking rate from 16% to 27%"

Numbers are the trust signal. Generic claims ("saves time and money") are invisible noise.

### 4. Spec-Driven Process Description
Several top profiles describe *how they work*, not just what they build. Muhammad U.'s "full technical spec before coding begins" is a process differentiator that signals professionalism and reduces client risk perception.

### 5. Narrow Tool Stack in Title, Broad Industries in Bio
Titles are narrow (specific tools, specific role). Bios are broad (many industries served). This combination ranks for specific searches while signaling versatility to browsing clients.

### 6. Portfolio: Outcome-Framed Titles
Portfolio item titles that include measurable outcomes significantly outperform generic titles. "50-char title limit, 500-char description limit" per item. Examples of strong framing:
- "Dental Clinic: +93 Bookings in Week 1 via Voice AI"
- "FinTech: 70% Reduction in Manual AR Tracking"

### 7. Skill Tags — Fill All 20 Slots
All 20 available skill tag slots should be filled with niche-specific terms. Each tag = a potential search match. Top performers mix: broad (AI Automation, LLM Integration) + specific (n8n, Make.com, Claude API, RAG, LangChain, Claude Code, MCP).

### 8. Productized Services (Project Catalog)
Several top performers have Project Catalog listings alongside their main profile — fixed-price packaged services like "How to use Claude Code in your business — consultation." This creates a low-friction entry point and shows up in separate search contexts.

### 9. Differentiation From Generic "AI Consultant"
How top Claude/AI profiles separate from the generic crowd:
- **Name the model:** "Claude Code" not just "AI"
- **Name the tool:** "n8n" not just "automation"
- **Name the industry:** "Healthcare," "SaaS," "Real Estate" — not just "businesses"
- **Cite a process:** spec-first, production-ready, "no demos"
- **Anti-hype positioning:** "I push back against AI hype" — trust signal

1. **Hook (outcome statement)** — first 2 sentences
