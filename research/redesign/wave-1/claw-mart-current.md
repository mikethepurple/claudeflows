# Claw Mart — Current State Check (April 16, 2026)

**Research date:** April 16, 2026
**Prior research date:** March 2026 (DEMAND-VALIDATION-REPORT.md)
**Source:** shopclawmart.com, TrustMRR (Stripe-verified), OpenClaw.report, Mixergy interview (March 27, 2026)

---

## Key Findings

1. **Revenue has nearly doubled since March.** Total Stripe-verified revenue is now $159,921 (was $86K in early March). Last 30-day revenue: $51,404 — implying a sharp acceleration.

2. **The "one seller" thesis is broken.** The homepage now shows 6 distinct seller accounts with meaningful sales: Felix Craft (3,002 sales), Brian Wagner (1,035), Dima Vogel (259), GeoffGuides (128), Clarence Maker (108), Atlas Forge (39). Third-party supply/demand is real now.

3. **Claw Mart is #1 on TrustMRR's OpenClaw leaderboard** — overtaking prior position (#3 in late March per Nat's own tweet). $51K in the last 30 days places it ahead of AI MONEY GROUP ($31K), Roofclaw ($15K), and Coral ($12K).

4. **The marketplace now has 20+ products across 13+ categories.** March research found ~14 listings (6 personas, 8 skills). The current catalog has expanded with new sellers bringing independent products across Engineering, Marketing, Sales, Research, Finance, and Executive categories.

5. **Nat Eliason's direct fingerprints have faded from the storefront.** The homepage no longer lists Nat by name — products sell under brand personas (Felix Craft, Clarence Maker, Convrgent). The underlying brand is OpenClaw ecosystem infrastructure, not personal brand.

6. **MRR subscription revenue exists.** 277 active subscriptions generating estimated $6,020 MRR — a recurring revenue layer that didn't exist in the March snapshot.

### Pricing

No structural pricing changes detected. The price architecture remains:
- Entry-level skills: $1–$9
- Mid-tier personas: $19–$49
- Premium personas/bundles: $59–$99
- Creator economics: 90% rev share, 10% to Claw Mart
- Creator subscription: $20/month (referenced in multiple sources, enables API publishing)

The $20/month creator subscription is a new revenue lever not prominent in March analysis. At 277 active subscriptions, even if only a fraction are creator subs (vs. consumer), it adds meaningful MRR.

### OpenClaw vs. Claude Ecosystem

Claw Mart remains 100% OpenClaw-focused. Zero pivot toward Claude Code, Claude Projects, or Anthropic's official Claude Marketplace (launched March 6, 2026 — enterprise B2B play). The Claw Mart "about" page pitches compatibility with "OpenClaw, Claude Projects, Custom GPTs, Cursor and other instruction-friendly AI tools" — but OpenClaw is the anchor.

The competitive context has intensified. The OpenClaw ecosystem now has 183+ verified startups on TrustMRR generating $201K+/month collectively. ClawHub (3,200+ curated skills, 28 categories) is emerging as a competing marketplace. OpenClaw itself has 230K+ GitHub stars and 116K+ Discord members.

## Actionable Takeaways

1. **The marketplace model is validated faster than expected.** $159K total, $51K in 30 days, from a standing start ~2 months ago. For ClaudeFlows, this confirms the model works — the question is Claude Code ecosystem size vs. OpenClaw ecosystem size.

2. **Third-party supply does bootstrap if the platform is active.** Brian Wagner at 1,035 sales and Dima Vogel at 259 show independent sellers finding buyers. The March assumption that "third-party creator economy appears near-zero" was wrong 6 weeks later.

3. **Subscription layer ($20/month creator, 277 subs) is a meaningful unlock.** ClaudeFlows should consider a creator subscription tier — API access, featured placement, analytics — not just pure rev share. Claw Mart appears to be generating ~$5.5K/month from subscriptions alone.

4. **Nat's personal brand abstraction into "Felix Craft" is instructive.** The marketplace didn't die when it de-personalized. Consider whether ClaudeFlows needs a founder-personality anchor or can run on ecosystem brand from day one.

5. **Price architecture ($1–$99 one-time) holds.** No move to recurring/subscription on the consumer side. Skills are cheap impulse buys ($1–$15); complete personas are considered purchases ($49–$99). This matches what we'd expect for ClaudeFlows.

6. **OpenClaw ecosystem has compressed competitive moat.** ClawHub (3,200+ skills, 28 categories) is emerging. Claw Mart's defensibility is: first mover, brand trust, Felix's public story, and creator payouts milestone. For ClaudeFlows, first-mover in the Claude Code ecosystem matters — but Claude Code's audience is more developer-concentrated than OpenClaw's.

7. **Press comes through founder story, not marketplace announcement.** No tech press on Claw Mart qua marketplace. All media covered "Nat Eliason gives an AI $1K and it makes $300K" — the human story drove the traffic. ClaudeFlows launch strategy should have a similar narrative hook.
