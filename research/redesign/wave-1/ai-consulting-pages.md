# AI Consulting Pages — Research Wave 1

Research date: 2026-04-16
Scope: 9 consulting/productized-service pages across AI, automation, and developer tools verticals

---

## Key Findings

### 1. Pricing transparency is a differentiator, not a liability
Solo consultants with visible prices (Promptful, NurtureKit, ZeroCodeNinja) outperform the "contact us for a quote" model for attracting inbound traffic. Agencies hide prices; solo practitioners who show them get more qualified leads. Transparent pricing signals confidence and filters out tire-kickers.

### 2. Three-tier card layouts dominate the conversion layer
Nearly every effective page uses a horizontal 3-card pricing layout with a "Popular" or "Recommended" badge on the middle tier. The pattern: Entry (low-risk, accessible) → Main (most margin, highlighted) → Premium (high-ticket, custom). This is the single most copied pattern across all verticals.

### 3. "Book a Free Call" beats all other CTAs
Every high-converting page anchors to a free discovery call as the primary CTA — not a form, not an email. Most use Calendly embed or direct link. The call removes purchase friction for high-ticket services. Pages place it above the fold, after the process section, after testimonials, and in the footer — typically 3-5 times per page.

### 4. Process sections are mandatory trust-builders
Every page uses a numbered "How We Work" section (3-5 steps). This is the section that converts skeptics. Clients don't know what they're buying — a process section answers "what actually happens after I click book." Pages without one feel incomplete and untrustworthy.

### 5. Metrics trump logos for solo operators
Agencies use client logos (Makeitfuture: 250+ clients, 6000+ automations; Automaly: £100K+ revenue unlocked). Solo practitioners use outcome stats ("100+ workflows built," "15 hours/week saved per client," "Booked out for 3 months"). Either works — but something quantified must appear before the pricing section.

### 6. Comparison tables convert skeptics
Goodspeed's "Us vs. DIY" table is the standout technique. Rather than just selling themselves, they position against the actual alternative (doing it yourself), which is what most prospects are actually considering. This framing works especially well for developer tools where the prospect thinks "I could build this myself."

### 7. Solo vs. agency: different credibility stacks
- **Solo**: Personal bio, headshot, specific outcome stats, testimonial screenshots, "working directly with me" language. Price transparency is expected.
- **Agency**: Partner badges (Zapier Official, Make Silver, HubSpot), ISO certifications, client logos, scale metrics (automations delivered, clients served). Price is hidden, custom quote required.

ClaudeFlows sits between these — solo credibility (Misha's name, direct access) + productized packages (agency-style fixed tiers). This hybrid is relatively uncommon and should be played up.

## Detailed Analysis

### 1. ZeroCodeNinja — zerocodeninja.com/ai-automation-pricing/
**Vertical:** n8n/AI automation  
**Structure:** Straight to pricing (no hero fluff), 3-card horizontal layout, FAQ, footer CTA  
**Above the fold:** Headline + subheading + pricing cards, no scroll required  
**Packages:** Starter Workflow / Growth System (Popular) / Automation Partner — clearly named around outcomes not features  
**Trust signals:** None visible (no logos, testimonials, metrics) — weakness  
**CTA:** "Book a Call" button appears after cards + footer  
**Pricing:** No dollar amounts visible (complexity-dependent), but package names and inclusions are explicit  
**What works:** Fastest path to the offer of any page reviewed. No preamble. Pricing page = the offer.  
**What's missing:** Zero social proof, no metrics. Loses credibility by skipping trust entirely.

### 2. GrowthScribe (Kartik Ahuja) — growthscribe.com/zapier-expert/
**Vertical:** Zapier automation, solo consultant  
**Structure:** Hero → Benefits (4) → Portfolio → Process (3 steps) → Services → Use Cases → Testimonials → FAQ → Form  
**Above the fold:** Dark hero, "Hire a Zapier Expert" headline, green CTA ("Let's do it")  
**Packages:** Three service categories (automate tasks / fix broken zaps / consultation) as simple bullets, plus "$35/hour base rate" shown plainly  
**Trust signals:** "Certified Zapier experts," portfolio of recent projects, embedded testimonials  
**CTA:** Hero button → scheduling link ("Schedule a call with Kartik") → contact form x2  
**Pricing:** Partially transparent ($35/hr shown openly), custom quotes per project  
**What works:** Portfolio section with "Recent Projects" is highly credible for a solo operator. Process section (form → quote → execute) reduces anxiety.  
**What's missing:** Pricing cards. The hourly rate model doesn't communicate value as well as fixed packages.

## Actionable Takeaways for ClaudeFlows /consulting

### Page structure (in order)
1. **Hero:** Outcome-first headline + one quantified metric + primary CTA ("Book a Call" or "See Packages")
2. **Trust bar:** 2-3 signals — client count, skills built, outcome metric. No logos needed yet.
3. **Packages:** 3-card horizontal layout. Sprint ($500) → Full Setup ($2,000) → Architecture ($5,000+). Highlight the middle tier.
4. **How It Works:** 3-4 numbered steps. What happens after booking. Reduces anxiety.
5. **Retainer:** Separate card or callout below main packages. "Ongoing" tier stands apart.
6. **FAQ:** 5-6 questions. What's included, typical timeline, what if I need more, how billing works.
7. **Footer CTA:** Repeat booking link.

### Pricing transparency
Show all prices. ClaudeFlows' range ($500–$5K+) is accessible enough that hiding prices creates more friction than it avoids. Promptful's model (full transparent pricing page) is the right model. Architecture tier can say "$5,000+" without a fixed ceiling.

### CTA strategy
Primary CTA = Calendly link ("Book a Free 30-min Call"). Place it: hero, after packages, after FAQ. Don't use a form as the primary CTA — it reduces response speed and feels bureaucratic for a technical audience.

### Trust signals that work for a solo/early-stage operator
- X skills published in registry (specific number)
- X developers using ClaudeFlows (early number is fine, even 10)
- "Direct access to Misha" — solo credibility signal, not a liability
- One or two testimonials from beta users (even informal quotes)
- Avoid fake-looking ISO badges or inflated metrics

### What separates the good from the mediocre
- **The comparison table:** "Working with ClaudeFlows vs. building it yourself" — steal this from Goodspeed. Developer audience will respond to this framing.
- **Outcome metrics in the hero, not features:** "Setup in 2 hours, not 2 weeks" beats "we do AI consulting."
- **"See Packages" as hero CTA:** NurtureKit's aggressive buyer-assumption posture works. Treat visitors as buyers not browsers.
- **Floor price in the hero:** "Starting at $500" — Processter technique. Sets expectations before they hit the pricing section.

### Package naming
Avoid "Bronze/Silver/Gold" and "Starter/Growth/Enterprise." Use outcome-named tiers:
- **Sprint** (fast, time-boxed, specific problem)
- **Full Setup** (complete, guided, production-ready)
- **Architecture** (strategic, complex, long-horizon)
- **Studio** (monthly partnership, ongoing)

This naming maps to how ClaudeFlows buyers think about their needs, not how consultants think about effort.
