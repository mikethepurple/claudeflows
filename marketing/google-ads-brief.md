**Bidding strategy for a new account (no data):**
- Start both campaigns on **Maximize Clicks** with a max CPC cap.
- Set max CPC cap: $8 for Campaign 1, $4 for Campaign 2.
- This prevents runaway spend on broad terms while you gather data.
- Do NOT use Target CPA or Target ROAS until you have 15+ conversions in a 30-day window. Without data, Smart Bidding will underdeliver or overspend randomly.
- Switch to Target CPA for Campaign 1 only when you hit 15 consulting form submissions.

**Ad rotation:** Set to "Optimize" (Google picks best-performing headline combos). Do not use "Do not optimize" — with RSAs, Google needs permission to test.

## Conversion Tracking

Track these events in Google Ads (via Google Tag + GA4):

### Event 1: Consulting Form Submit (PRIMARY — highest value)
- **Trigger:** Thank-you page load after /consulting form submit, OR form submit event on the consulting page
- **Conversion value:** $50 (estimated lead value — adjust once you know your close rate)
- **Count:** One per click

### Event 2: Email Capture (SECONDARY)
- **Trigger:** Email gate submit on skill install
- **Conversion value:** $5
- **Count:** One per click

### Event 3: Skill Page View — 60+ seconds (MICRO)
- **Trigger:** GA4 scroll/time event — user on /skills/[any] for 60+ seconds
- **Conversion value:** $1
- **Count:** One per click (proxy for purchase intent)

### How to set up:
1. Install Google Tag (gtag.js) in your Next.js `_app.tsx` or via Vercel's built-in GA integration.
2. Create conversions in Google Ads → Tools → Measurement → Conversions.
3. Import the GA4 events you configured above.
4. Verify with Google Tag Assistant Chrome extension before going live.
5. Allow 24–48 hours for conversions to start appearing.

**Do not use auto-import of all GA4 events.** Hand-pick only the three above. Auto-import creates junk conversions that confuse Smart Bidding.

## Week 1–2 Playbook

### Day 1 — Launch
- Set both campaigns live with Maximize Clicks + max CPC caps.
- Confirm conversion tracking is firing (use Tag Assistant).
- Do not touch anything else for 48 hours.

### Day 2–3 — First Data Check
- Check Search Terms report (Campaigns → Insights & reports → Search terms).
- Add irrelevant terms to negatives immediately.
- Look for: searches that are clearly job-seeking ("claude code jobs"), free tool hunting ("free claude code"), or off-topic ("claude monet").
- Do not pause keywords yet — you need impressions first.

### Day 4–5 — Impression Share Check
- If a keyword has 0 impressions in 5 days, it likely has no search volume yet. Pause it.
- If impression share is below 30% on a good keyword, your bid cap is too low. Raise it $1–2.
- Check Quality Score for each keyword (1–10). Anything below 5 needs ad copy or landing page work.

### Day 7 — First Optimization
- Pause the lowest CTR headlines in each RSA (Google shows headline performance in the ad assets report).
- Review which ad groups are spending their budget. If Campaign 1 is underspending, raise the max CPC cap by $1.
- Check: are you getting any consulting form submissions? If yes, note which keywords drove them.

### Day 8–10 — Search Term Mining
- The Search Terms report now has enough data to be meaningful.
- Export it. Sort by impressions. Move any good exact-match terms into explicit exact match keywords.
- Add more negatives from irrelevant terms.

### Day 11–14 — RSA Performance
- Google needs ~2 weeks to test headline combos.
- Check the "Ad strength" score — aim for "Good" or "Excellent."
- If ad strength is "Poor," you likely have too many similar headlines. Vary the angles.
- Do not rewrite ads entirely at day 14. Make surgical edits (swap 1–2 headlines).

**Warning signals that mean pause and investigate:**
- Avg CPC exceeds $12 on Campaign 1 consistently
- CTR below 1.5% after day 7 (means ads aren't resonating)
- Zero impressions on any keyword after 5 days (too niche or bid too low)
- Spend burns out by noon every day (budget cap too tight for good traffic)

## Expected Performance — Honest Numbers

At $15/day with no prior account history, no Quality Score, no conversion data, here is what to expect:

### Month 1 (Learning Phase)
- **Campaign 1 (Consulting):** 1–3 clicks/day at $6–10 avg CPC. Maybe 20–40 clicks total in month one. At a 5–10% form submit rate, that's 1–4 consulting leads. One closed deal at $500+ makes the month profitable.
- **Campaign 2 (Skills):** 3–8 clicks/day at $2–4 avg CPC. 60–150 clicks in month one. At a 2–5% email capture rate, that's 2–8 email subscribers. Skill sales from this channel in month 1: likely 0–3.

**Total month 1 spend:** ~$450
**Realistic leads:** 1–4 consulting inquiries, 60–150 site visits
**Realistic revenue:** $0–2,000 (depends entirely on whether one consulting lead closes)

### Month 2–3 (Optimization Phase)
- Quality Scores improve, CPCs drop 10–30%.
- You'll have conversion data to feed Smart Bidding.
- Expect consulting leads to increase as the account builds history.
- Skills traffic should compound as you add negative keywords and refine match types.

### What $15/day cannot do:
- Dominate a keyword. Your budget runs out in hours on competitive terms.
- Build brand awareness. Google Search is intent-only at this spend level.
- Replace organic/content. Treat this as a supplement to SEO and community posts, not a replacement.

### CTR benchmarks for developer tools (2026 industry reference):
- Branded terms: 15–30% CTR
- Competitor terms: 3–8% CTR
- Generic/informational terms: 1.5–4% CTR
- Consulting/service terms: 3–8% CTR (high intent = higher CTR when the ad matches well)

### The honest math:
At $15/day, you are buying signals and data more than you are buying customers. By month 3, you will know: which keywords drive consulting intent, what your actual CPC is for your market, and whether search ads are a viable channel at all. If consulting leads are closing at $500–5K, even one lead per month from ads justifies the spend. If skills are driving $9–99 purchases, you need 5–50 purchases/month to break even on $450 spend — that requires a significantly higher traffic volume than this budget can generate alone.

**Recommendation:** Keep Campaign 1 (consulting) running permanently. Evaluate Campaign 2 (skills) at 60 days based on email capture cost-per-lead. If email CPL exceeds $20, pause and redirect that $6/day to Campaign 1.
