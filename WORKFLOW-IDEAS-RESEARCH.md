# Claude Code Workflow Ideas: Market Research (March 2026)

Research conducted across Reddit, Upwork/Fiverr, Product Hunt, Twitter/X, Google Trends, Notion marketplace, Zapier/n8n templates, and existing SaaS competitors.

## 1. Competitor Intelligence Report Generator

**What it does:** Takes a company name or URL, produces a structured competitive analysis: company overview, product positioning, pricing tiers, strengths/weaknesses, market share estimates, recent news, SWOT matrix, and strategic recommendations.

**Target user:** Marketing Manager, Product Manager, Strategy Consultant, Startup Founder

**Evidence of demand:**
- Fiverr: competitor analysis gigs priced $45-$95 per report (multiple sellers with 100+ reviews)
- Existing SaaS: Competely.ai, Crayon ($499/mo), Visualping ($50/mo), Semrush ($139/mo)
- Upwork: "competitive analysis" is a recurring search term with hundreds of active jobs
- n8n/Zapier: competitor monitoring is among most popular automation templates
- Market: AI competitor analysis tools range from free to $3,000+/month

**Why pay vs DIY:** Manual competitor analysis takes 8-20 hours per competitor. Most professionals don't know how to structure one properly. Existing SaaS tools cost $50-$500/month for ongoing monitoring, but many people just need a one-time or quarterly deep dive.

**Price point:** $49 (single report) or $99 (5-company competitive landscape)

**Feasibility:** HIGH. Claude excels at structured research synthesis. With web search MCP, it can pull real data. Output is text + structured tables — perfect for Claude Code skill format. Already proven by the scout workflow.

## 2. SEO Content Brief Generator

**What it does:** Takes a target keyword + business context, produces a comprehensive content brief: search intent analysis, recommended headings/structure, word count target, semantic keywords to include, competitor content gap analysis, internal linking suggestions, and a first-draft outline.

**Target user:** Content Marketing Manager, SEO Manager, Agency Content Strategist

**Evidence of demand:**
- Frase.io: $15-$45/month, built entire company around this one workflow
- Surfer SEO: $89-$129/month, content brief is core feature
- Clearscope: $149/month for 100 queries
- Fiverr: SEO content briefs and keyword research gigs are among top-selling categories
- Upwork: "AI content strategy" and "SEO content plan" are high-demand skill areas

**Why pay vs DIY:** Writing a proper content brief requires keyword research, SERP analysis, competitor content auditing, and semantic analysis. Most marketing managers skip it because it takes 2-4 hours per brief. Bad briefs produce bad content that doesn't rank.

**Price point:** $29 (10 briefs/month) or $79 (unlimited)

**Feasibility:** HIGH. Claude can analyze search intent, suggest structure, identify semantic clusters, and produce detailed outlines. With web search, it can analyze competitor pages. The output is pure text — ideal for Claude Code.

## 10. Startup Financial Model Narrative Generator

**What it does:** Takes key business assumptions (pricing, growth rate, CAC, churn, team plan) and produces: a 3-year financial narrative with revenue projections, unit economics analysis (LTV:CAC, payback period), sensitivity analysis on key variables, investor-ready assumptions page, and a plain-English executive summary of the financial story.

**Target user:** Startup Founder (Pre-seed to Series A), CFO, Financial Analyst at early-stage company

**Evidence of demand:**
- Slidebean Accelerate: $42/month includes Financial Modeling Bootcamp
- PitchBob: $29.90-$99 for pitch deck + financial narrative
- 88% of startups using AI-generated pitch materials saw increased investor engagement
- Professional pitch deck services cost $3,000-$10,000 (designer) or $6,000+ (agency)
- Fiverr: financial modeling gigs consistently priced $100-$500

**Why pay vs DIY:** Most founders can build a spreadsheet but can't write the narrative that makes investors understand the business model. The "assumptions page" and "sensitivity analysis narrative" are where most pitch decks fail. A consultant charges $2,000-$5,000 for this.

**Price point:** $79 (single model narrative) or $149 (includes quarterly updates)

**Feasibility:** MEDIUM-HIGH. Claude can do the narrative and analysis well, but won't produce a working spreadsheet. The skill would produce the text/narrative layer that goes alongside a financial model — the part most founders skip.

## 12. Contract Review Summary for Non-Lawyers

**What it does:** Takes a contract (text pasted in) and produces: plain-English summary of key terms, risk flags with severity ratings, unusual or missing clauses, comparison to standard market terms, negotiation talking points, and a checklist of items to discuss with a lawyer.

**Target user:** Startup Founder, Freelancer, Small Business Owner, Operations Manager

**Evidence of demand:**
- LegesGPT: starts at $1 (massive demand signal — ultra-low friction)
- goHeather: $99/month for AI contract review
- Spellbook: growing fast in small law firm market
- LegalFly: targeting small firms and solo practitioners
- Ironclad: enterprise at $60K+/year (showing large enterprise demand too)
- Market grew from $812M (2023) to $887M (2024) and continues expanding
- Reddit: contract review is among top tasks people use ChatGPT for

**Why pay vs DIY:** A lawyer charges $300-$600/hour for contract review. Most freelancers and small business owners sign contracts without proper review because they can't afford a lawyer for every agreement. An AI-powered plain-English summary bridges this gap.

**Price point:** $29 (3 contracts) or $79/month (unlimited)

**Feasibility:** MEDIUM-HIGH. Claude is excellent at reading and summarizing legal text, identifying risk terms, and explaining jargon in plain English. Caveat: must include clear disclaimer that this is not legal advice. Text-only deliverable.

## Key Insight: Pricing Sweet Spot

Most competing SaaS tools charge $30-$150/month for ongoing subscriptions. Claude Code skills have a unique positioning opportunity:

- **One-time use skills** at $29-$79 (vs $150+/month SaaS)
- **Volume bundles** at $79-$149/month (vs enterprise SaaS at $500+/month)
- The value prop is: "Get the output without the subscription"

## Key Insight: Non-Technical User Fit

All 12 workflows above target non-technical professionals and produce text deliverables. The Claude Code skill format (text in, text out) maps perfectly to what these users need — they don't want dashboards or integrations, they want the document/deliverable itself.
